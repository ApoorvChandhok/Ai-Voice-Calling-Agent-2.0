Do first

Competitor deep-dive

Study Retell AI, Bland AI, Vapi.ai, Synthflow, Air.ai — pricing, features, positioning, API docs
Read every G2/Capterra review — find where they fail and where customers complain
Sign up for their free tiers and actually test call quality, latency, and dashboard UX
Map their pricing: per-minute billing, seat-based, flat SaaS — understand unit economics
Do first

Technology landscape research

LLMs: Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini — benchmark latency and cost for real-time voice conversation
STT (speech-to-text): Deepgram, AssemblyAI, Whisper — latency and accuracy for Indian accents
TTS (text-to-speech): ElevenLabs, Cartesia, Play.ht, Deepgram — naturalness and latency
Telephony: Twilio, Vonage, Plivo (good for India) — per-minute cost, SIP integration
Real-time infra: WebSockets, WebRTC, low-latency streaming pipelines
Do first

Customer discovery (most important)

Interview 15–20 businesses in 2–3 target industries about their phone operations
Key questions: How many calls/day? What % are repetitive? What does a human agent cost?
Quantify their pain: if they spend ₹8L/year on call center, your ₹2L/year saves them ₹6L
Best starting niches for India: real estate, ed-tech, healthcare clinics, D2C e-commerce, lending
Research before building

Regulatory & technical constraints

TRAI regulations for commercial calling in India (DND registry, consent frameworks)
IT Act 2000 and DPDP Act 2023 — data localization requirements for voice recordings
HIPAA equivalent considerations if targeting healthcare clients
Permissible calling hours and disclosure requirements ("you are speaking with an AI")
Don't try to build everything Retell AI has on day 1. Start with one use case (e.g. inbound customer support for one industry) and do it exceptionally well.
MVP — months 2–4

Core product to build first

Inbound call handler — AI picks up the phone, understands intent, responds naturally
Agent builder — no-code interface to define agent's persona, goals, and knowledge base
Call dashboard — live transcripts, recordings, call summaries, sentiment analysis
Phone number provisioning — let clients connect their existing numbers or buy new ones
Call transfer to human — escalation logic when AI can't handle the query
Phase 2 — months 4–7

Growth features

Outbound batch calling — upload a lead list, agent calls them all automatically
Appointment booking — integrate with Google Calendar / Calendly to book slots live
CRM integrations — HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho CRM (huge for Indian market) push call data
Webhook / API — let developers integrate your agent into their own flows
Hindi + regional language support — massive differentiator in India

Core model

Pricing structure to adopt

Per-minute billing (like Retell): ₹3–8/min of AI call time — predictable, scales with usage

White-label tier: agencies pay ₹50K–₹2L/month to resell your platform under their brand
Enterprise custom: flat ₹2L–₹10L/month for large call centers — negotiated contracts

Key metrics to track

Unit economics to obsess over

Gross margin: your cost per minute (API costs) vs what you charge — target 60–70% margin
CAC (customer acquisition cost) vs LTV — LTV should be 3x+ CAC minimum
Monthly churn — keep below 3%; if clients churn it means the product isn't delivering ROI
Time-to-value — how fast can a new client get their first AI call live? Target under 48 hours
First 90 days

Getting your first clients

Offer free 30-day pilots to 5 businesses in your chosen niche — get testimonials and data
LinkedIn outreach to founders/ops heads in your target industry — be specific about their pain
Ironically, cold call businesses using your own AI agent to demo the product
Join Delhi NCR startup/founder WhatsApp groups and communities — word of mouth is powerful
Build a case study after every client — quantify calls handled, agents replaced, cost saved
Months 3–6

Channel strategy

Partner with digital marketing agencies — they have SMB clients who need AI calling
Partner with no-code agencies (Make, n8n, Zapier builders) who can integrate your product
Content marketing: YouTube demos showing "AI agent handling 100 calls while founder sleeps"
Build in public on LinkedIn/X — post weekly updates, call recordings (with consent), metrics
Launch a certified partner program — other agencies resell your product for 20–30% revenue share
Months 6–12

Scale channels

SEO content: "AI receptionist for clinics", "automated lead follow-up India" — high intent keywords
Google/Meta performance ads targeting business owners in your niche verticals
Speak at industry events — BNI, FICCI, startup conferences relevant to your target industry
List on marketplace integrations: Zoho marketplace, Salesforce AppExchange, HubSpot marketplace
Legal is not optional — one TRAI violation can get your phone numbers blacklisted and kill the business. Consult a startup lawyer early.
Before you launch

Business registration & structure

Register as Private Limited Company (not proprietorship) — needed for enterprise clients and funding
Get GST registration — SaaS products attract 18% GST
Trademark your brand name before you launch publicly
Draft standard MSA (Master Service Agreement) and SLA templates for clients
Critical

Telecom & AI calling regulations in India

TRAI mandates: caller must disclose it's an AI/automated system — build this into every agent
DND (Do Not Disturb) registry compliance — scrub your calling lists before outbound campaigns
Commercial calls allowed only 9am–9pm, customer consent required for certain categories
Register as a Telemarketer with TRAI if doing outbound campaigns at scale
Data & privacy

DPDP Act 2023 compliance

Collect explicit consent before recording any call — implement consent prompts in agents
Data localization: store Indian user call data on Indian servers (AWS Mumbai / GCP Mumbai)
Right to erasure: build ability for users to request deletion of their call recordings and data
Publish clear Privacy Policy and Terms of Service covering AI data usage

When you're at ₹50L+ ARR

Sales team (2–3 AEs) + SDRs for outbound — especially for enterprise segment

Compliance & legal counsel — especially if expanding to healthcare or BFSI
If you're a solo founder, your first task is to find a technical co-founder or a strong engineering agency partner. You cannot build voice AI infrastructure alone while also doing sales.
